# My First Outline

## Creating Engaging Events for Developers
by Anna Korol, Associate Developer Advocate, Developer Relations

### Intro
What do we mean by Developer Events? Explain how the idea of internal events for devs was born initially from the Cloud Enablement team and eventually went on to form a whole Developer Relations team. Maybe shortly interview either Philip Haugaard or Rasmus Hald to get the backstory.
### Why do Developers Need Events?
Explain why it's important to connect colleagues, encourage learning, and foster a true sense of community among our developers.
### The Challenge of Engagement 
Introduce the challenges of keeping devs entertained and engaged pre, during, and post events. Maybe interview Lasse Christiansen on his experience with collaborating with the LEGO Group on various internal AWS events. Introduce contests and ways to engage the audience by raffling off LEGO sets and providing treats during events...because when there's cake, there's people
### Building Trust
We don't want to oversaturate and blast the inboxes of every dev and be put on their internal blacklist. It's important to foster trust with the developers and let them know that we are on their side to advocate for their needs. What better way to understand their needs than to personally interact with them. Our Lead Engineer, Tao Hansen, and Senior Engineer, Lily Chan, recently took a trip to the LEGO London Hub to do just that. Interview Lily and Tao and get their experiences of travelling and talking directly to users.
### Event Planning 101
What does it take to plan an event? From smaller workshops, to DevFairs, to global internal conferences - DevRel has done it all! Learn about the logistics needed to plan events. Some organization skills necessary, but passion for developer happiness is the key to success!
### A Little Creativity Never Hurt Anybody
Explain the power of graphic design to elevate marketing and promotional materials for events. It helps that we get to work with the best brand in the world, making it easier to create exciting materials.
### Feedback is your new best friend
The golden key to mastering the art of events is to talk to your audience. Feedback is the most crucial part of hosting events, so you know how to improve. Feedback is a gift and we treat it as such to make sure the developer's voice is heard and acknowledged. 
### Keeping the flame lit
How do we keep the streak going and consistently bring fantastic events time after time? The answer is that every event can't be wildly successful and that's okay. There's many factors that come into play, such as not enough promotion, too many events going on at the same time or even a topic that may not have been as popular as initially thought. It's important to move forward and build a sense of consistency for the community. 
### Conclusion
It's been a fantastic journey in bringing a culture of knowledge sharing to our colleagues at the LEGO Group. As a team, we are proud of the work that we do and the value that Developer Relations brings to our developers.
After all, as a Developer Relations team, we do exist to make devs happy and their daily workflow better. Cheers to more happy devs! We are onto our next challenge: scaling an internal Developer Conference to bring together three hubs.
